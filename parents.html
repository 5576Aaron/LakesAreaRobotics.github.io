Parent Page

FIRST Robotics Competition or FRC is a program for high school students working with adult mentors to learn about STEM and even some other aspects of teamwork and operating a robotics club. For more information, visit our About FIRST page. Iowa City Robotics is a non profit organization that was setup to support the Iowa City area FRC team, #167 - Children of the Corn.

The Iowa City FRC program runs year round and is split into 4 seasons:

  • Off-Season (May-Aug): During this time meetings are more sporadic and event-based. We focus on outreach events, fundraisers, and host robot camps for 7-10 year olds.
  • Pre-Season (Sep-Dec): The team meets once weekly to recruit and train new FRC members
  • Build Season (Jan-Mar): This is the most critical time for the team. In this two months the competition game is revealed and the robot is constructed. Team meetings will be more frequent.
  • Competition Season (Mar-Apr): The team travels for 1-2 regional events to hopefully qualify for the world championship.



SEASON BREAKDOWN

The FRC game changes every year and the kickoff of a new season is typically the first full weekend in January. This is a virtual event but we typically get together and watch it as a team. Kickoff is also an important day for students to breakdown the rules of the game and begin forming their strategy.

During the build season, we typically meet 3-4 times a week. As we get closer to our competition date, the frequency of meetings increases. On the weekends we tend to get a couple of pizzas to keep the students energy levels high. We also love it when parents donate snacks for the team. The mentors are determining what dates work best for our schedule. You can always find our practice schedule here.



The team typically registers to compete in 2 regionals. Regional competitions are 3 days long, a practice day followed by two days of matches. The regionals are livestreamed but parents are welcome and it is free to the public. For the 2022 season, our first regional is in Duluth, Minnesota. We will travel to Duluth on Wednesday March 1, Thursday is the practice day and Friday/Saturday is match play and the playoffs. We will travel back on Sunday, March 5. The other regional we are competing in is the Iowa Regional which is in Cedar Falls. That one, we will travel up on Thursday morning, March 22. Again, Friday and Saturday is match play and playoffs but we will travel back that Saturday evening. If we are fortunate to qualify for the World championships, that competition is in Houston on April 19-22.

These competitions are such a great experience where all our hard work is put into motion. Although competitions are not required, it is encouraged. Once you've been to one, you always want to go back. Estimated costs for these competitions are typically $100-$200 per student. This will cover transportation and lodging along with our competition uniform (polo and t-shirt). If we continue to fundraise, some of these costs can be covered.

WHAT IS NEEDED FROM PARENTS

  • Register your student in STIMS if you haven't done so already. It's required every year and it doesn't carry over from one FIRST program to another. If registered for an FTC team, you still need to register for our FRC team. You can do this by going to firstinspires.org and click on register in the top right corner of the website. If you need help, go to this site. Our team number is 167 and the program is FRC or FIRST Robotics Competition. This is a requirement for students wanting to participate in any FIRST competition.
  • Complete the parent/guardian liability waiver and health forms. Please indicate if there's any food allergy that we need to be aware of. Have your student turn in a hard copy or email Coach Mike an electronic version. These are due before the build season starts.
  • We have a team apparel on-line store where you can purchase our team competition polo shirt if your student doesn't have one already and a team competition t-shirt that is new every year. We also have sweatshirts and a quarter zip available for purchase. Team store orders are due December 12. You can visit the store here.
  • Any help connecting with company sponsors or donors. We have raised enough to cover registration fees and some robot material costs. We need a lot more if we want to cover student competition costs.
  • When shopping on Amazon, consider using smile.amazon.com and choose Iowa City robotics as your charity. We get 0.5% on all purchases.
